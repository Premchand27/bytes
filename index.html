About

Learn More About Us

The Department of Information Technology is launching a newsletter, "Bytes," which encompasses state-of-the-art development in IT industries, competitive programming nuggets, cutting-edge problem-solving techniques, and student and faculty achievements. This unique newsletter creates a platform for alma mater to contribute articles on best industry practices. This approach helps current students to get acquainted with the latest technology. Active participation in alma mater is the primary goal of this newsletter. It serves the department and institution growth twofold. Firstly, the industry's best practices can be reflected in our student community through their alums. It makes more sense to the students. Secondly, it helps to bridge the gap between industry and academia. It's the dream of any computer science/Information Technology graduate to join companies like Meta, Apple, Google, etc. The newsletter devotes a particular column to algorithm design techniques and their importance in MAANG companies. "Bytes" will act as a platform to nourish coding techniques to crack such companies. We invite our research collaborators and academicians to share a glimpse of fundamental research to enable our faculty and students to solidify their research quotient.

F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the "Bytes" newsletter about?

    The "Bytes" newsletter covers state-of-the-art developments in IT industries, competitive programming insights, cutting-edge problem-solving techniques, and highlights student and faculty achievements. It also includes contributions from alumni on best industry practices.

  • Who can contribute to the "Bytes" newsletter?

    Both current students and alumni are encouraged to contribute to the "Bytes" newsletter. We also welcome contributions from research collaborators and academicians who wish to share insights on fundamental research and industry best practices.

  • How often is the "Bytes" newsletter published?

    The frequency of publication for the "Bytes" newsletter can vary. Typically, it may be published on a monthly or quarterly basis, depending on the volume of content and contributions received.

  • What topics are covered in the "Bytes" newsletter?

    The newsletter covers a range of topics including the latest advancements in IT, competitive programming tips, problem-solving techniques, student and faculty achievements, algorithm design techniques relevant to MAANG companies, and fundamental research insights.

  • How can I subscribe to the "Bytes" newsletter?

    To subscribe to the "Bytes" newsletter, you can visit the Department of Information Technology's official website and sign up with your email address. Subscription details will also be available through departmental communications.

  • How does the "Bytes" newsletter benefit students?

    The "Bytes" newsletter benefits students by providing them with the latest industry insights, competitive programming tips, and problem-solving techniques. It also bridges the gap between academia and industry, helping students prepare for careers at top tech companies like Meta, Apple, and Google.
